<p class="poem-note">(After reading Philip Larkin's <em>Mythical Introduction</em>)</p>

<div class="verse">
an aria spray caresses
the vessel's blouse
bosom suffused with song

swelling, taut and drenched
propelling the mast
impelling ship and sailor
toward the ravishing bird
her succulent word:

come hither, seaman,
bowsprit and spar
to a tender haven
kiss the salty hair
of a windblown raven
rhythmic chords guide

to a craggy strand
the swaying cords lure

to a hidden shoal
a perilous harbor

a hydraulic troll
a tentacled womb
cloaked in seabed sand
⋮

a wanting depth-sound

the betrayal of man
the desperate prayers
the rope-torn hands
the fragmented hull
the shipwrecked seafarer
shivers in cephalopod lair
the predatory mollusk's

testicular hood
intelligent leer
eight-armed cervix
and parrot-like beak
a ravenous thrust

a singular shriek
ribbons of arterial blood

wrap the sirenopus feast
roll in the hymenal ocean
lap the shoreline's rosy crust

and sea dog's shredded husk
⋮

the desiccated severed skull

has for years been mounted
near the brackish inlet
it still appears surprised

at first love, transfixed
with a maiden's wide eyes
</div>
