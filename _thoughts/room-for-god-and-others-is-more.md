*Room for God and Others* is more vision than discursive thought. I imagine the cover is a watercolor painting of a country road. There is a feeling of goodness and honoring God. The book is not an argument, but a story, even though there is no evidence at all that I am capable of telling a story. *Room for God and Others* is a memoir about science and story, women and reading, or what? I do not know.
